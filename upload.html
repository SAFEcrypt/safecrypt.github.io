Files uploaded to the network are broken into pieces, encrypted and distributed across the network. This process is called Self-Encryption.
When a user uploads (or saves) a ﬁle to the SAFEbox, via SAFEcrypt Drive APP, the ﬁle is automatically broken up into chunks. These chunks are then encrypted (encoded so that only authorised parties can read it), randomised and stored on your data drive. These encrypted chunks are completely unreadable and inaccessible to anyone other than the owner.
